Albums

Alkaloid are a band that values quality over quantity. Since their formation in 2015, they have released just three albums. Each one is the result of a slow, deliberate creative process. Rather than rushing to stay visible, they take the time to refine their sound, pushing for depth, complexity, and originality in every release. This commitment to quality has earned them critical praise and a loyal following within the metal community, who recognize that with Alkaloid, the wait is always worth it. Let us dive into each of their three albums to see how the band has evolved—and why each release stands as a distinct artistic milestone.

The Malkuth Grimoire

Alkaloid’s debut The Malkuth Grimoire set the tone for everything that would follow: ambitious, technical, and unafraid to experiment. It introduced listeners to their unique blend of progressive death metal infused with avant-garde elements. The album’s sprawling compositions, complex rhythms, and surreal themes immediately established Alkaloid as more than just another side project—it was a bold new voice in extreme music. Standout tracks include Carbon Phrases and Cthulhu.

Liquid Anatomy

With Liquid Anatomy, Alkaloid expanded their palette, embracing even greater dynamics and contrast. The record flows between crushing heaviness and moments of delicate atmosphere, weaving in influences from jazz, fusion, and classic prog. The result is an album that feels more fluid and adventurous, showcasing the band’s ability to evolve while maintaining their unmistakable identity. It proved that Alkaloid were not content to simply replicate their debut, but intent on pushing themselves further with each release. Highlights include Chaos theory and practice and Rise of the cephalopods.

Numen

Their most recent offering, Numen, is the culmination of years of growth. It is darker, more focused, and perhaps their most cohesive work to date. The songwriting feels sharper, the performances tighter, and the conceptual depth more immersive. While still brimming with technical brilliance, Numen channels that skill into songs that strike as much with emotion as with complexity. It is the sound of a band fully confident in their vision, delivering an album that feels both monumental and deeply personal. Key tracks include Clusterfuck and The Cambrian Explosion.
